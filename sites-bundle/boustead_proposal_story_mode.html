Flow and architecture, simplified for stability

1) Edge-first detection

RTSP/ONVIF streams are pre-processed, sampled, and screened at the edge with YOLO/RT-DETR. Only meaningful candidates move forward.

2) Candidate + policy validation

Confidence, zone matching, repetition suppression, and schedule/time rules gate the critical path before centralized AI and human workload.

3) Resilient queue and closure

Event queue with retry policy and DLQ discipline protects operations during bursts and network instability.

4) SmallVLM + decision layer

Uncertain frames go through SmallVLM for contextual checks; DGX Spark + policy/RAG returns recommendation quality with consistent escalation behavior.

5) HITL closure layer

Operators acknowledge, choose outcomes, and close with auditable notes: False Positive, Resolved On Site, Escalated To Patrol, Drone Verification Required.

6) Thermal risk lane

Smoke/fire candidates in night and haze risk zones are cross-verified with thermal, reducing ambiguity before escalation.

Visualization strategy and critique (implemented for narrative clarity)

The objective is not more charts; it is one claim at a time. The page uses one focal view, then supporting context panels.

Primary visual: Threat-to-Closure funnel

Claim: critical alerts are not just detected, they are converted to accountable closure actions.

Design: simple ordered bars with visible state labels for AI Flagged → Acknowledged → Resolved/Escalated.

Why: easy read without hover or dashboard familiarity.

Secondary visual: Zone risk map summary

Claim: unresolved threats concentrate in high-risk zones that need staffing priority.

Use an operator-first card order by zone, then map context as a visual anchor only where geography changes interpretation.
