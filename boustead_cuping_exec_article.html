Problem and outcome, in 30 seconds

Boustead Cuping handles a 400-acre estate with mixed environmental and access risks. Current alarm patterns can fail because detection is only one step of the process, while human response ownership is often unclear.

This proposal applies a tighter operating contract:

  • AI generates prioritized physical-threat flags from camera and thermal inputs where useful.
  • Humans receive accountable tasks, acknowledge them, and close each case by one of four outcome types.
  • Every step is auditable with confidence, zone, severity, assigned user, and timestamp.

Commercial reason to proceed now

A faster detection line without ownership does not reduce risk. This model reduces delay at the front end and prevents unresolved incidents at the back end.

6-step operating flow (required)

1. Detect

Edge cameras and risk filters identify physical threat candidates.

Input: optical + thermal where installed
2. Validate

Policy filters remove noise and enrich with zone, confidence, severity.

Output: ranked event list
3. Assign

Event is assigned to accountable user and queue owner.

Output: owner + deadline
4. Acknowledge

Operator confirms visibility and starts action path.

Output: SLA clock started
5. Resolve / Escalate

Choose False Positive, Resolved On Site, Escalated To Patrol, or Drone Verification Required.

Output: final human action
6. Audit

Closure is stored with evidence and timestamped trail.

Output: reviewable record

Threat coverage and thermal confirmation

Threat taxonomy (phase 1)

  • Fire / Smoke / heat anomalies
  • Human intrusion and access-route movement
  • Wild boar and wildlife entry
  • Vehicle intrusion
  • Loitering and restricted-zone entry
  • Arson-risk night movement review

Thermal detector role

  • Thermal is an assist lane, not a replacement.
  • Optical flags in smoke/fire zones are cross-checked with thermal signals.
  • Candidate confidence can be strengthened before escalation.
  • Ambiguous high-risk alerts can route to patrol or drone verification.

Human-in-the-loop as a commercial guarantee

Physical threat alerts must not disappear after AI detection. Every physical threat must keep a named owner, an active response state, and recorded outcome.

Acceptance gates and executive KPI controls

These are the minimum gates for board-level acceptance and pilot continuation:

% of critical AI flags acknowledged within SLA

Shows responsiveness in high-risk periods and proves operational readiness.

% incidents resolved with evidence

Confirms incidents are truly closed and reviewable.

Median human validation time

Validates workforce capacity and queue health under load.

Open unresolved physical threats by zone

Prevents hidden risk concentration in vulnerable sectors.

Repeat unresolved threat locations

Signals blind spots and repeat vulnerability in zone controls.

Operational quality gates

Duplicate alert rate, event-to-queue stability, and critical miss rate remain part of the same scorecard.

Governance and risk controls

What changes now
  • Only candidate-worthy events enter operator queue.
  • Every critical alert requires response or escalation.
  • All closures are timestamped and auditable.
What is not included in this cycle
  • Backend deployment changes.
  • Database/API redesign or new platform services.
  • New model procurement not already scoped.

Rollout cadence (single-page view)

  • Phase 1: Scope lock, roles, permissions, and KPI definitions.
  • Phase 2: 2-3 week controlled pilot in highest-risk zones.
  • Phase 3: Evidence and closure review with repeatability tests (fixed clips + live rotation).
  • Decision gate: Pilot scale-out only if acknowledgment, closure, and unresolved-risk gates are met.

Decision ask for Boustead leadership

  • Approve phase-1 pilot with the 6-step flow as business policy.
  • Approve thermal-assisted fire risk verification in the pilot zones.
  • Approve KPI acceptance gates and audit retention format before any wider rollout.

Outcome after 2-3 weeks: measurable reduction in unresolved physical threats, auditable closure behavior, and board-ready risk evidence.
